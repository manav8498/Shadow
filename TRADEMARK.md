# Trademark policy

The name **"Shadow"** and any associated logos are **not** covered by
the MIT or Apache-2.0 licenses that govern the source code. The licenses
grant you freedom to use, modify, and redistribute the *code*, they do
not grant you permission to use the *name* in a way that could confuse
users about origin or endorsement.

## You can

- Write about Shadow, review it, cite it, integrate with it, fork it.
- Say "this tool is compatible with Shadow" or "this is a fork of Shadow."
- Distribute unmodified binaries named `shadow`.
- Keep the name in the filenames / module names of a fork for a
  reasonable transition period.

## Please don't

- Call your fork "Shadow" in a way that suggests it is the official project.
- Use the name for a commercial product, SaaS offering, or company name
  without written permission.
- Modify the code and then redistribute under the name "Shadow" without
  clearly marking it as a modified version (e.g. "Shadow-fork-by-X").
- Use the Shadow name or logo in a way that implies endorsement by the
  Shadow project.

## When in doubt

Open a discussion or email the current maintainers (see
[MAINTAINERS.md](./MAINTAINERS.md)).

## Future

If Shadow is ever transferred to a neutral foundation (CNCF, Linux
Foundation, etc.), the trademark will transfer with it and this policy
will be updated accordingly.

## Standard OSS practice

This policy follows the standard CNCF pattern, see, e.g.,
[Kubernetes's policy](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/trademark-usage/)
or [Rust's policy](https://foundation.rust-lang.org/policies/logo-policy-and-media-guide/).
Nothing here is novel.
